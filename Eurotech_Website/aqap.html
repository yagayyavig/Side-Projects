Allied Quality Assurance Publications

About AQAP 2110 / AQAP 2310

The Allied Quality Assurance Publications (AQAP) 2110 and AQAP 2310 are NATO-developed standards that set forth contractual quality requirements for defense-related suppliers. These standards are essential for companies working with NATO member nations, especially in aerospace, military manufacturing, and defense logistics.

AQAP 2110 focuses on quality assurance for design, development, and production, while AQAP 2310 is tailored specifically for software quality assurance. Both require the implementation of a Quality Management System (QMS) aligned with ISO 9001, with additional NATO-specific clauses for configuration control, risk management, and audit readiness.

Key Components of AQAP 2110 / 2310

  • Configuration Management: Control and verification of product and software configurations through structured change and audit trails.
  • Design and Development Control: Defined methods for verifying that systems meet design intent and NATO requirements.
  • Supplier Control: Requirements for subcontractor evaluation, NATO quality clause flow-down, and surveillance.
  • Software Verification (2310): Validation, testing, code reviews, and traceability for software used in military systems.
  • Risk and Opportunity Management: Identification and mitigation of risks impacting contract performance, safety, or reliability.
  • NATO Quality Assurance Representative (NQAR): Compliance with third-party inspections and audits as required by NATO agencies or defense procurement offices.

Why AQAP Certification Matters

  • Mandatory for contracts with NATO member armed forces and procurement authorities.
  • Ensures compliance with defense quality expectations and audit protocols.
  • Improves product consistency, documentation, and risk control in military-grade systems.
  • Enhances eligibility for large-scale government and defense projects globally.
  • Demonstrates reliability and commitment to defense sector clients.
