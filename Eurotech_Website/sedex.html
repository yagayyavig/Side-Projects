SEDEX Audit

Evaluates ethical practices using the SMETA

About SEDEX (SMETA) Audits

SEDEX (Supplier Ethical Data Exchange) is a global membership organization focused on driving ethical improvements in global supply chains. Companies using SEDEX typically undergo SMETA (Sedex Members Ethical Trade Audit), which is one of the most widely used ethical audit formats in the world.

The SMETA audit evaluates a company’s performance in key areas such as labor rights, health & safety, environment, and business ethics. While SEDEX itself is not a certification, it provides a platform to share SMETA audit data with potential clients and stakeholders in a transparent, secure way.

Key Components of a SMETA Audit

  • Labor Standards: Evaluates compliance with labor laws, wages, working hours, contracts, and freedom of association.
  • Health and Safety: Assesses on-site safety, emergency preparedness, sanitation, and employee welfare.
  • Environmental Management: Reviews waste handling, emissions, resource usage, and environmental impact policies.
  • Business Ethics: Investigates practices to prevent corruption, bribery, and unethical conduct.
  • Management Systems: Ensures that policies and procedures exist for continuous improvement in ethical operations.
  • Site Tour and Worker Interviews: Includes visual inspections and confidential interviews with workers.
  • Audit Sharing: Allows members to share reports securely with buyers via the SEDEX platform.

Why SEDEX/SMETA Audits Matter

  • Demonstrates your organization’s commitment to ethical sourcing and fair labor practices.
  • Meets buyer and retailer requirements for ethical compliance across global markets.
  • Improves supply chain transparency and accountability.
  • Enhances your company's reputation and appeal to CSR-conscious clients.
  • Reduces the risk of reputational damage, worker dissatisfaction, and noncompliance penalties.
  • Facilitates long-term partnerships with international brands and buyers.

Eurotech Canada supports businesses in preparing for and undergoing SMETA audits, helping them register with SEDEX, align with global ethical standards, and maintain ongoing compliance.
