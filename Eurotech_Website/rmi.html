Assesses sourcing policies for conflict minerals

About RMI Certification

The Responsible Minerals Initiative (RMI) provides companies with tools and resources to responsibly source minerals like tin, tungsten, tantalum, and gold (commonly referred to as 3TG). These minerals are often linked to conflict and human rights violations in high-risk areas such as the Democratic Republic of Congo and surrounding regions.

RMI certification helps organizations demonstrate due diligence in their mineral sourcing practices by aligning with international frameworks like the OECD Due Diligence Guidance. It is an essential component of ethical supply chain management and is widely recognized across industries including electronics, automotive, and aerospace.

Key Components of RMI Compliance

  • Conflict Mineral Reporting Template (CMRT): Standardized tool for collecting and disclosing sourcing information from suppliers.
  • Due Diligence Program: Implementation of robust policies and processes for identifying and managing risks in mineral supply chains.
  • Risk Assessment: Evaluation of country of origin and supply chain risk based on geopolitical and human rights factors.
  • Auditable Records: Maintenance of documentation supporting mineral origin, supplier practices, and smelter/refiner status.
  • Engagement with Smelters/Refiners: Encouragement of participation in RMAP (Responsible Minerals Assurance Process) to verify ethical sourcing.
  • Annual Reporting: Public disclosures submitted to customers, stakeholders, and regulatory bodies as required.
  • Alignment with Global Standards: Compliance with OECD guidance, EU Conflict Minerals Regulation, and U.S. Dodd-Frank Act Section 1502.

Why RMI Certification Matters

  • Demonstrates ethical responsibility and transparency in mineral sourcing.
  • Meets buyer expectations and corporate social responsibility (CSR) goals.
  • Supports compliance with U.S. and EU regulations on conflict minerals.
  • Reduces reputational and legal risks in global markets.
  • Encourages responsible practices across the supply chain.
  • Improves stakeholder trust and investor confidence.
  • Facilitates access to business opportunities with leading manufacturers.
