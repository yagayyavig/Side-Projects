About EN 15085 Certification

EN 15085 is the European standard for the welding of railway vehicles and components. It applies to manufacturers involved in design, production, and maintenance of railway systems where welding is a critical process. Certification under EN 15085 ensures a high level of safety, reliability, and quality in welded assemblies used in rail applications.

Whether you produce rolling stock frames, brake systems, or structural components, EN 15085 certification is essential for market access in the European railway industry and is often a prerequisite in public tenders and international contracts.

Key Components of EN 15085 Certification

  • Certification Levels: Manufacturer Certification Levels (CL1 to CL4) based on the complexity and criticality of welding tasks.
  • Welding Coordination Personnel (WCP): Appointment of qualified staff (IWE/IWT) to supervise and ensure welding quality.
  • Welding Procedure Qualification (WPQR): Validation of specific welding processes used during production.
  • Welder Qualification: Certified welders under EN ISO 9606 performing tasks within their approved scope.
  • Quality System Requirements: Integration of welding quality into the manufacturerâ€™s quality management system (e.g., ISO 9001).
  • Traceability & Documentation: Proper documentation of materials, processes, inspections, and final acceptance criteria.
  • Third-Party Audit: Certification conducted by a Notified Body or recognized third-party authority in railway welding.

Why EN 15085 Certification Matters

  • Ensures safety and structural integrity of welded railway vehicles and parts.
  • Grants access to contracts with European rail operators and OEMs.
  • Demonstrates technical competency and compliance with EU welding standards.
  • Reduces risk of weld failures and enhances product reliability.
  • Improves customer trust and boosts competitiveness in global railway markets.
  • Supports integration with other certifications such as ISO 3834 and ISO 9001.
  • Essential for manufacturers bidding on high-value railway infrastructure projects.
