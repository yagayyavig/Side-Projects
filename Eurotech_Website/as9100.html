AS 9100 D Certification

Aerospace Standard for Quality Management

About AS 9100 D

AS 9100 D is the globally recognized quality management system standard specifically developed for the aerospace, defense, and aviation industries. It builds upon the ISO 9001 framework, while adding industry-specific requirements to address safety, reliability, and traceability throughout the aerospace supply chain.

The standard is published by SAE International and maintained by the International Aerospace Quality Group (IAQG). AS 9100 D applies to manufacturers, suppliers, and service providers involved in design, development, and production of aerospace components or systems.

Key Components of AS 9100 D

  • Product Safety: Emphasizes proactive risk analysis and the prevention of conditions that could lead to unsafe operations or products.
  • Counterfeit Part Prevention: Introduces controls for ensuring authenticity and traceability of parts, materials, and components.
  • Configuration Management: Ensures products conform to approved design and documentation throughout the lifecycle.
  • Operational Risk Management: Systematic identification and mitigation of risks affecting quality and delivery in aerospace operations.
  • Customer Satisfaction & Requirements: Enhanced focus on meeting regulatory and customer-specific quality requirements (e.g., FAA, EASA).
  • Validation & Verification: Strong emphasis on process validation, first article inspection (FAI), and production process controls.
  • Supply Chain Oversight: Flow-down of requirements to external providers with evaluation and performance monitoring.

Why AS 9100 D Matters

  • Mandatory for many aerospace manufacturers and Tier 1 suppliers worldwide.
  • Helps demonstrate commitment to quality, reliability, safety, and airworthiness.
  • Improves operational consistency and reduces production and service risks.
  • Supports global market access through recognition by aviation OEMs (e.g., Boeing, Airbus, Lockheed Martin).
  • Minimizes defects, rework, warranty claims, and potential liabilities.
  • Facilitates inclusion in the Online Aerospace Supplier Information System (OASIS) database.
