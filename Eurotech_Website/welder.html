About CE Certification for Welder Testing

Welder testing and qualification are essential parts of ensuring the integrity, safety, and compliance of welded structures and components. Under EU standards such as EN ISO 9606 and EN 287, CE certification for welder testing validates a welderâ€™s ability to produce sound welds according to specific procedures and materials.

Whether for pressure vessels, steel structures, or railway applications, certified welders are vital for maintaining product reliability and meeting industry-specific CE marking requirements. This section outlines the components and benefits of CE certification for welder testing.

Key Components of CE Welder Certification

  • Applicable Standards: Conformance with EN ISO 9606 series and EN 287 standards for welder qualification testing.
  • Welding Procedure Specification (WPS): Defined welding parameters that must be followed during qualification.
  • Qualification Test: Supervised practical weld test witnessed by a Notified Body or approved examiner.
  • Testing & Evaluation: Non-destructive and destructive testing of test pieces (e.g. radiography, bend test) as required by the standard.
  • Welder Qualification Record (WQR): Documented proof of successful qualification including scope, process, and validity.
  • Periodic Renewal: Welder certificates must be renewed periodically with continued practice and performance verification.
  • CE Compliance Linkage: Essential for products requiring CE marking, especially under EN 15085 and EN 1090 standards.

Why CE Welder Testing Matters

  • Demonstrates technical skill and reliability of individual welders.
  • Reduces the risk of welding-related failures in critical infrastructure.
  • Mandatory for structural steel, pressure vessels, and railway vehicle fabrication.
  • Enhances quality control and compliance with CE directives and harmonized standards.
  • Improves job readiness and employability of certified welders.
  • Required for CE-marked welded components under EN 1090 and EN 15085.
  • Builds client trust and reduces project risks during inspections and audits.
